## Conflict of interest
I would be remiss to mention that I have previously worked for CSIRO in 2013 - 2014. I did my Honours research year and started my PhD at the [Australian Centre for Disease Preparedness](https://www.csiro.au/en/about/facilities-collections/acdp) [1] (previously AAHL). My PhD supervisor was cut during the [2014-2015 budget cuts](https://www.ibtimes.com.au/tony-abbott-140m-csiro-budget-cuts-hamper-australian-research-1342569) [2], resulting in a well-funded lab group of ~10 people losing their jobs, impacting several ongoing industry-funded collaborations and I was personally displaced in the process and had to move all my work to Monash University in 2015. Safe to say I am still salty about this.


No matter how many times CSIRO [proves itself](https://www.csiro.au/en/about/achievements/Top-10) [3], the [consultant-addicted](https://www.consultancy.com.au/news/3213/federal-government-spend-on-big7-consulting-firms-tops-1-billion) [4], fossil-fuel gargling ghouls in Australia's federal government continue to cut their budget. Importantly, this impacts highly skilled experts in key strategic areas including 102 jobs in [climate](https://www.smh.com.au/environment/climate-change/worst-thing-i-ve-ever-seen-csiro-slashes-climate-modelling-jobs-20260310-p5o97a.html), [110 in health/biosecurity, 55 in agriculture/food and 35 in minerals](https://www.governmentnews.com.au/csiro-departmental-cuts-revealed/) [5, 6]. Despite a recent boost of $223 million AUD from the federal Australian government, job cuts will go ahead as planned.


My feeling is that this is ideological given the targeted nature of job areas, but let's dig into the facts to demonstrate why these job losses are being made by people with a few kangaroos loose in their top paddock.
